Dear Editors,

I am pleased to submit the manuscript titled *“Quantum Pressure and Phase Modulation in the Genesis Field: Resolving Cosmic Acceleration and Hubble Oscillations”* for consideration in *Physical Review D*, under the section “Cosmology, Gravitation, and Field Theory.”

This work introduces the Genesis Field, a first-principles cosmological model in which late-time acceleration arises from vacuum coherence—specifically, quantum pressure and global phase modulation. Derived from a covariant extension of the Gross–Pitaevskii equation, the model predicts ripple-like oscillations in the Hubble parameter \( H(z) \), grounded in the curvature of the vacuum potential. No new scalar fields or modifications to Einstein's equations are introduced.

The ripple signature is not phenomenologically imposed: it is hand-derived (handwritten derivations included in Github) via the Madelung transformation applied to a coherent vacuum wavefunction, resulting in an observable modulation of the expansion rate. All derivations were developed from scratch and are provided in Appendix A and in fully documented form in the accompanying GitHub repository:
  
**https://github.com/mrrgreene/genesis-field-paper1-phase-modulation**

The model is empirically tested against Pantheon+ Type Ia supernova and cosmic chronometer data. It exhibits strong fits under constraint, with ripple suppression when disfavored and emergence when permitted. The model reduces smoothly to ΛCDM and introduces a sharply testable prediction in the range \( 0.5 < z < 2.0 \), to be probed by Euclid, Rubin, and JWST.

This paper includes reproducible code, an operational glossary, and serves as the first in a five-paper series exploring how gravity, matter, time, and constants may emerge from quantum coherence. To my knowledge, this is the first cosmological model to derive late-time acceleration observables from internal phase dynamics of a coherent quantum vacuum—anchored in known analogs from Bose–Einstein condensates.
